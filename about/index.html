This project examines how secessionist and restorationist movements constitute a “people,” and where the rhetorical machinery that does so holds or fails. It extends Maurice Charland’s 1987 study of the peuple québécois across three cases: Québec sovereignty, Alberta’s 2026 referendum, and the United States “true America” framing. The through-question is whether the people a movement calls into being can carry the future it was made for, and can occupy the ground it claims.

Status

All writing on this site is the author’s working draft and subject to revision. Empirical claims are grounded in verified, resolving sources recorded in the Literature Review. No figures, quotes, or citations are fabricated. Where a load-bearing claim could not be verified it is omitted or marked unverified inline.

Citation guidance

To cite a page from this site, use the format below, substituting the page title and its canonical URL:
